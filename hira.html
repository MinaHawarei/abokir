The High Institute of Restoration of Antiquities

About the institute

The Higher Institute for Tourism, Hotels and Antiquities Restoration was established by Ministerial Decree No. 1435 of 2000 and by Decision No. 618 of 2001 of the Higher Council of Universities. On 18 August 2018, the B.A. degree was equivalent to the B.A. degree in Archaeological Restoration awarded by the Institute. (Restoration of antiquities) granted by Egyptian universities subject to University Regulation Act No. 49 of 1972 and its implementing regulations.

Restoration of monuments:

The Department is concerned with the study and maintenance of archeological materials, whether organic or inorganic, or with them chemically and mechanically, in order to preserve them for as long as possible, while ensuring that the piece does not lose the historical value and artistic form on which they originally existed and thus preserve the different cultural values. The Department is also concerned with the scientific and technical aspects of civilizations and heritage in general. ‏

Institute Departments:

The Institute awards the degree of Bachelor of Antiquities (Restoration of Antiquities) in the following disciplines where the Division is selected from the third division of the Institute.

Restoration of non-organic buildings and artefacts :

The study (restoration of buildings - restoration of inorganic artefacts - minerals, ornaments, pottery stones - glass. . etc.)

Restoration of organic activities :

It aims at graduating students with the following knowledge and skills (wood and furniture restoration - leather and textile restoration).

Restoration of paintings :

It aims at graduating students familiar with the various knowledge associated with the restoration of paintings (restoration of murals, mosaics, objects, oil paintings and Puerto Rican icons).

Quality Assurance Unit

About the unit

The Quality Assurance Unit was established in the Board of Directors session that was held on Saturday, March 28, 2015. The Quality Assurance Unit succeeded in adopting the academic reference standards in April of 2017 and the academic reference standards contain four basic axes upon which the student is graduated, namely:

  1. Knowledge and understanding
  2. Mental skills
  3. Skills: Professional and practical
  4. General and transitional skills
‏

Dr ./D. Wael Abdel Basset, faculty member of the Institute, was appointed Director of the Unit and assigned to contact the National Authority for Quality Assurance of Education and Accreditation in Cairo to complete accreditation procedures.

Vision

Upgrading the status of the Higher Institute for Tourists and Hotels, restoring monuments at the national and regional levels, contributing to overall quality and high performance, and maintaining continuous improvement in the output of the educational process and community service.

the message

The Quality Assurance Unit seeks to disseminate a culture of quality and to monitor and evaluate the performance of all components of the educational system and service activities provided by the Institute, in the light of the standards of the National Authority for Quality Assurance of Education and Accreditation.

Committees for implementing and following up the activities of the Quality Assurance Unit:

    The committees for implementing and following up the activities of the Institute's Quality Assurance Unit consist of eight committees:
  1. Media and Training Committee.
  2. The Academic Courses and Standards Committee.
  3. Follow-up and evaluation committee.
  4. Questionnaires Committee.
  5. Crisis and Disaster Management Committee.
  6. Labs and classrooms committee.
  7. Student Committee and Alumni Affairs.
  8. Examinations Committee.

Certifications and accreditation

The duration of the study is four years, in which the decisions of the Higher Council of Universities include the granting to graduates of the Institute of a bachelor's degree in the speciality of restoration of antiquities awarded by the Institute with a bachelor's degree in antiquities. The Institute is the only one in Egypt to be awarded a bachelor's degree in archeological restoration and a certificate approved by the Ministry of Higher Education.
